We're interested in how people understand physics. You will be shown several movies depicting physical simulations of objects interacting. The physics in these simulations does not necessarily work like the real world. Your task is to figure out how it works.

The objects in these movies come in 2 types: blue and red. Each object can be either heavy, or light. Also, each object type can potentially exert forces on other types: object types either attract, repel or don't interact with one another.

You will be viewing the objects from above, moving on a two-dimensional plane, so you can think of them as "hockey pucks". This plane contains green and yellow surfaces. Surfaces can be either sticky or smooth.

Finally, there may or may not be a global force, pulling all pucks in a particular direction: north, south, east or west).

Each animation begins with a brief pause on an image like what you see above, where the arrows show the direction of the initial velocity of the pucks. If an object does not have an arrow next to it, its initial velocity is zero.

In the next pages you will be shown *** 5-second movies of pucks moving. Each movie is drawn from a different world, and the physics may differ from movie to movie. For each movie you will be asked the same questions about the physics of the situation.

If the movie doesn't contain enough information to make a decision, or if you are uncertain, make you best guess given the data.
